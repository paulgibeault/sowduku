How to play

The rules

Settle exactly one piggy per row, per column, and per colored pen. A fourth rule gives Sowdoku its shape — The Wallow Rule: no piggy may touch another, not side by side, not even corner to corner. Every field has exactly one solution, reachable by pure logic. If you find yourself guessing, back up and look again.

Controls

Tap a cell to settle a piggy — tap it again to lift it. Long-press (or right-click) fences off a cell with a hoofprint, your "definitely-not-here" note. Drag paints hoofprints across a whole run at once. ⌘Z undoes the last gesture. 👁 peek (in the action bar) glows the next logically-forced piggy when you're stuck. On a keyboard, arrow keys move around the board, Enter settles or lifts a piggy, and Space marks a hoofprint.

Difficulty

Four bands, each asking a little more of the same rules: Sunbeam (forced singles), Meadow (a few confinements), Hilltop (at least one contradiction chain), Crag (several, sometimes nested).

Stakes

How strictly a heart is docked, from most to least forgiving: slow — no hearts, no failure, just the ritual. gentle — a heart only for a placement that breaks a rule against pigs already down. honest — also a heart for a piggy that's legal right now but not where it truly belongs, or for fencing a whole pen shut with your own hoofprints. stern — also a heart for fencing off the one cell a piggy actually belongs in.

Assist

Chosen once, when you tend a field (in the ✚ create sheet), not a mid-game toggle. On softly shades cells a pen can no longer use, on any difficulty. Off leaves all the bookkeeping to you.
