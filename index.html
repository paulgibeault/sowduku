How to play

The rules

Settle exactly one piggy per row, per column, and per colored pen. A fourth rule gives Sowdoku its shape — The Wallow Rule: no piggy may touch another, not side by side, not even corner to corner. Every field has exactly one solution, reachable by pure logic. If you find yourself guessing, back up and look again. A heart is docked for a placement that breaks a rule against pigs already down — nothing stricter than that, unless a particular field says otherwise (the Wallow, for one, holds every mark to closer account).

Controls

Tap a cell to settle a piggy — tap it again to lift it. Long-press (or right-click) fences off a cell with a hoofprint, your "definitely-not-here" note. Drag paints hoofprints across a whole run at once. ⌘Z undoes the last gesture, always free. Peek (in the action bar) glows the next logically-forced piggy when you're stuck — it trades a heart for the hint, and never asks for a heart you can't spare. On a keyboard, arrow keys move around the board, Enter settles or lifts a piggy, and Space marks a hoofprint.

Difficulty

Four bands, each asking a little more of the same rules: Sunbeam (forced singles), Meadow (a few confinements), Hilltop (at least one contradiction chain), Crag (several, sometimes nested). One notch past Crag on the slider sits the Wallow — the knottiest Crag fields, played with one heart, no assist, and every mark held to account. Toggle misty weather alongside any of these for pens that hide until their piggy settles.

Assist

Chosen once, when you tend a field (in the ✚ create sheet), not a mid-game toggle. On softly shades cells a pen can no longer use, on any difficulty. Off leaves all the bookkeeping to you.
